About Me

I am a Mechanical Engineer with expertise in computational fluid dynamics for reacting flows, combustion modeling, heat transfer, and multiphase thermal-fluid systems. My research focuses on developing data-driven CFD methodologies to reduce computational costs for turbulent reacting flows.

Currently, I'm integrating machine learning frameworks (JAX, PyTorch, TensorFlow) with commercial CFD software through custom extensions, enabling faster design iterations while maintaining physics-based fidelity. I have extensive experience developing finite-volume solvers in OpenFOAM (C++) for thermal-fluid applications.

My work has been published in leading journals like Combustion and Flame, and I've presented at prestigious conferences including the APS Division of Fluid Dynamics and National Combustion Meeting.
